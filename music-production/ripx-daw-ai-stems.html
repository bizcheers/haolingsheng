The "Un-Production" Tool

RipX DAW isn't a traditional digital audio workstation; it's a **spectral editor**. While traditional DAWs treat audio as flat waveforms, RipX breaks audio down into its fundamental harmonic components, allowing you to edit it as if it were MIDI.

Note-Based Spectral Editing

Once you import a stereo file into RipX, it uses AI to "rip" the track into layers. You can then:

  • Pitch Correct Individual Notes: Fix a flat note in a recorded vocal without affecting the backing instruments.
  • Change Melodies: Click and drag the notes of a guitar solo to create a completely new melody from existing audio.
  • Clean Up Noise: Surgically remove unwanted sounds (like a door slam) from a recording by deleting its spectral signature.

Workflow: From Stereo to Stem

RipX excels at taking a completed "master" and turning it back into a project file. For remixers, this is the ultimate tool for extracting high-fidelity acapellas and instrumentals that traditional phase-cancellation can't reach.
