Changing Your Apple Watch Ringtone

Unlike the iPhone, the Apple Watch has a more "locked down" audio system. However, you can still control how your watch alerts you.

  1. On your iPhone, open the Watch App.
  2. Tap My Watch > Sounds & Haptics.
  3. Ensure "Silent Mode" is OFF to hear your ringtone.
  4. Note: The Apple Watch primarily mirrors your iPhone's ringtone for calls, but for notifications, you can choose specific patterns under "Haptic Alerts".

Customizing Your Morning Alarm

The default "Radar" alarm is scientifically designed to be stressful. Here is how to swap it for a song or a peaceful Zen Tone.

iPhone Method:

  • 1 Open the **Clock** app and tap on an alarm.
  • 2 Tap **Sound**.
  • 3 Scroll to the top and select **Pick a song** to use any music from your library.

Choosing the Right Wake-Up Frequency

Musicologists suggest that "pulsing" sounds are better for waking up the brain than constant drones. Our **Morning Alarm Pack** uses 120bpm rising melodies that gradually increase in volume, simulating a natural sunrise.

  • Ideal frequency: 500Hz to 2000Hz (The human voice range).
  • Ideal rhythm: 4/4 time at walking pace (100-120bpm).

Sleep Tracking Warning

If you use "Sleep Mode" (Wind Down) on iPhone, your custom alarm settings might be overridden by the "Sleep Schedule" sounds. To fix this, change your sound within the **Health App > Sleep > Your Schedule**.
