How to Delete Ringtones on iPhone

Accidentally added a 5-minute song as a ringtone? Or just clearing the clutter? Deleting ringtones on iOS is easier than ever.

The Modern Method (iOS 13 - iOS 18)

Apple used to require a computer and iTunes to manage ringtones, but that changed years ago. You can now delete custom tones directly within the Settings app.

Step-by-Step Removal:

  1. Navigate to Settings > Sounds & Haptics > Ringtone.
  2. Scroll past the default Apple tones to find your Custom tones.
  3. Swipe Left on the ringtone you want to remove.
  4. Tap the red Delete button that appears.
Note: You can only delete Custom ringtones (ones you added yourself). Default Apple ringtones (like Reflection or Opening) are part of the system and cannot be removed.

What if "Swipe Left" doesn't work?

If you don't see the delete option, your ringtone might have been synced using an older version of iTunes or a 3rd party tool. In this case:

  • Sync with Finder: Connect your iPhone to a Mac, open Finder, click on your device, and navigate to the "Tones" tab to manage or delete files manually. Sometimes a fresh sync will clear ghost ringtone files.
  • GarageBand Cleanup: If you created the ringtone in GarageBand, go to the "My Songs" browser in GarageBand, find the project, and delete it there as well.

Storage Fact

Custom ringtones are very small (usually under 1MB). Deleting them won't significantly increase your phone's storage, but it will make choosing your next sound much faster and less confusing!
