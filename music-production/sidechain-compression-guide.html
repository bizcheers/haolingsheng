The Most Famous "Pump" in Music

Sidechain compression is a mixing technique where the signal level of one track is controlled by the volume of another. It's the secret behind the "pumping" sound in EDM, but it's also a vital tool for clarity in every other genre.

The Kick and Bass Battle

The most common use of sidechain is to make room for the kick drum. When the kick drum hits, the sidechain compressor "ducks" (lowers) the volume of the bass for a fraction of a second. This prevents the low-end from becoming a "muddy" mess and makes the kick feel much more punchy.

Creative Uses Beyond the Kick

  • Vocal Clarity: Use the lead vocal to slightly duck the background guitars or synths. This pulls the vocal "forward" in the mix.
  • Reverb Ducking: Sidechain a heavy reverb to the dry vocal. This keeps the vocal clear while the reverb only fills the "gaps" between words.
  • Extreme Pumping: In genres like House or Future Bass, you can use extreme sidechain settings to create a rhythmic, lung-like "beat" in the pads and melodies.

The **Release** time is the most important setting for sidechain. It should be fast enough that the volume returns to normal before the next beat, creating a musical rhythm that matches your song's BPM.
