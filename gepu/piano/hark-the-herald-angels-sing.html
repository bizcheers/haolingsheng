📖 Historical Background

Hark the Herald Angels Sing — The music for 'Hark the Herald Angels Sing' was adapted from a festive chorus by Felix Mendelssohn (1840), combined with words by Charles Wesley (1739). Mendelssohn himself initially objected to the pairing. The triumphant melody has made it one of the most popular Christmas hymns worldwide.

Composer: Felix Mendelssohn (1840).

🔬 Musical Structure

In 4/4 with a grand, triumphant character. The two-voice piano arrangement features a bold melody in the right hand over a harmonically rich left-hand part. The refrain ('Hark the Herald Angels') is particularly fanfare-like.

🎵 Piano Performance Tips

  • This carol should feel celebratory and bold.
  • Keep a noble, unhurried pace.
  • The left-hand chords should provide solid harmonic support without overpowering the melody. Practice the eighth-note passages in the refrain to ensure even articulation.
