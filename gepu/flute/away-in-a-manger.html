📖 Historical Background

Away in a Manger — 'Away in a Manger' appeared in print in 1885 in a Lutheran Sunday school book. The tune most commonly used in the US (attributed to James Murray) and the UK (William Kirkpatrick) differ slightly. It remains one of the most beloved children's Christmas carols.

Composer: Traditional Carol (c.1885).

🔬 Musical Structure

The melody is in 3/4 time with a gentle, lilting quality. It moves smoothly by steps and small leaps, staying within a comfortable range for beginner flutists. The phrases are symmetrical and easy to memorise.

🎵 Flute Performance Tips

  • Focus on a warm, breathy tone for this lullaby-like carol.
  • Use a gentle vibrato on longer notes.
  • Pay careful attention to the 3/4 time signature—count 1-2-3 steadily and resist rushing the beat.
