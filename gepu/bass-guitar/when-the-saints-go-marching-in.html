📖 Historical Background

When the Saints Go Marching In — 'When the Saints Go Marching In' is a traditional American spiritual that became a New Orleans jazz standard, famously associated with Louis Armstrong. Its simple, driving four-note opening motif and repetitive structure make it one of the most recognisable bass lines in popular music, and an ideal beginner piece for bass guitar.

Composer: Traditional American Spiritual (19th century)

🔬 Musical Structure

In 4/4 time with a driving, march-like character. The simple four-note ascending motif is one of the most recognisable in popular music. The bass guitar arrangement shows the melody and walking bass interplay that made this song famous in jazz.

🎵 Bass Guitar Performance Tips

  • Keep a steady, driving groove — this is jazz/gospel music and the rhythmic pulse is everything.
  • Use a firm, even finger-plucking technique. Keep the tone consistent across all strings.
  • Once comfortable with the melody, try adding simple passing notes between the main melody notes to create a walking bass feel.
