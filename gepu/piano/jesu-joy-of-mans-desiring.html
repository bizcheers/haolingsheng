📖 Historical Background

Jesu, Joy of Man's Desiring — 'Jesu, Joy of Man's Desiring' is the popular English name for the final chorale of BWV 147, composed by Johann Sebastian Bach in 1723. The flowing triplet accompaniment below the majestic chorale melody creates one of the most recognisable and beloved textures in Western music.

Composer: Johann Sebastian Bach (1723)

🔬 Musical Structure

In 9/8 time (or interpreted as 3/4 with triplet subdivisions), the piece features a flowing, continuous triplet figure in the right hand while the chorale melody emerges in longer note values. The simplified beginner arrangement captures the essence of this iconic texture.

🎵 Piano Performance Tips

  • The flowing triplet feel is the heart of this piece — keep it smooth and uninterrupted, like a gentle stream.
  • Bring out the longer melody notes clearly above the triplet accompaniment — they should sing above the texture.
  • Keep a gentle, consistent touch throughout. The piece should feel serene and meditative, never rushed.
