📖 Historical Background

Morning Has Broken — 'Morning Has Broken' is a Christian hymn with words written by Eleanor Farjeon in 1931, set to a traditional Gaelic melody called 'Bunessan'. It was popularised globally by Cat Stevens on his 1971 album 'Teaser and the Firecat'.

Composer: Eleanor Farjeon / Traditional Gaelic (1931).

🔬 Musical Structure

The piece is in 3/4 waltz time, giving it a gentle, flowing quality. The melody moves predominantly by steps, making it ideal for beginners. The Gaelic melody has a pentatonic quality that gives it a pure, timeless sound.

🎵 Ukulele Performance Tips

  • Focus on a smooth, connected style (legato) to capture the hymn-like character.
  • Use a light fingerpicking pattern rather than strumming to bring out the gentle melody.
  • Keep a steady pulse—do not rush the triplet feel.
