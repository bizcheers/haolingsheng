Routing for Success

Understanding the difference between **Inserts** and **Sends (Aux/Return)** is one of the most important milestones for a beginner producer. Using the wrong routing can lead to muddy mixes and a lack of control over your effects.

Inserts: The "Series" Connection

An Insert is placed directly in the signal path of a track. 100% of the audio goes through the effect. This is for processes that **change the fundamental character** of the sound:

  • EQ: You want to shape the whole sound.
  • Compression: You want to control the dynamics of the entire signal.
  • Distortion/Saturation: You are changing the texture of the source.

Sends: The "Parallel" Connection

A Send takes a "copy" of the audio and sends it to a separate "Aux" track. You then blend this processed copy back with the original dry signal. This is for **spatial and time-based effects**:

  • Reverb: This allows you to place multiple instruments in the same "room."
  • Delay: You can keep the dry vocal clear while the echoes sit in the background.
  • Parallel Compression: As discussed in our Parallel Processing guide!

üí° The "Bus" Secret

Using Sends for reverb and delay saves CPU power because you only need one instance of the plugin for multiple tracks. It also creates a more "cohesive" mix by making all instruments sound like they are in the same physical space.
