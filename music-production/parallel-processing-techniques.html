The Best of Both Worlds

Parallel processing (often called "New York Compression") involves blending a dry, unaffected signal with a heavily processed version of the same signal. This allows you to add power, weight, and grit without losing the "natural" dynamics and transients of the original sound.

Parallel Compression for Drums

Try sending your whole drum bus to an auxiliary channel. Apply a compressor with a high ratio (8:1 or more) and a fast attack to "obliterate" the transients. Then, slowly blend this "smashed" signal back into your main drum mix. The result? A drum sound that is both explosive *and* natural.

Parallel Saturation and Excitement

Instead of putting a saturator directly on a vocal, use a parallel bus. Distort the parallel signal heavily and filter out the low-end. When blended in, this adds "perceived loudness" and brightness that makes the vocal cut through a dense mix like a knife.

When using parallel processing, ensure your DAW's **delay compensation** is active. Even a tiny timing difference between the dry and parallel signals can cause "comb filtering," making your sound thin and hollow.
