Clearing the Mud

The High-Pass Filter (HPF) is the single most powerful tool for creating a clean, professional mix. Most instruments (including vocals and guitars) contain low-frequency "rumble" that isn't necessary for their sound but takes up valuable "headroom" in the mix.

The "High-Pass Everything" Myth

While clearing the low-end is important, over-filtering can make a mix sound thin and "sterile." The goal is to remove what is **unnecessary**, not what is **audible**.

  • Vocals: Usually safe to HPF up to 80Hz-120Hz.
  • Guitars: Can often be cut up to 100Hz to make room for the bass.
  • The 30Hz Rule: Many producers apply a steep HPF at 20Hz-30Hz on the entire master bus to remove sub-sonic noise that speakers can't even reproduce.

Fixed vs. Dynamic HPF

If a vocal sounds "thin" during some parts but "bassy" during others, use a **Dynamic EQ** or a multiband compressor instead of a static HPF. This allows you to tame the proximity effect only when it becomes a problem.
