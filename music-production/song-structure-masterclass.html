Escaping the 8-Bar Loop

Every producer has a folder full of "8-bar loops" that never became songs. The difference between a loop and a hit is understanding **how to structure a song**. Proper arrangement keeps the listener engaged and provides an emotional journey.

1. The Pop Standard (ABABCAB)

This is arguably **the most powerful song structure** in mainstream music. Verse-Chorus-Verse-Chorus-Bridge-Chorus. It‚Äôs familiar, catchy, and perfectly timed for radio and streaming.

2. The EDM Energy Flow

In electronic music, the focus is on tension and release. Intro -> Build-up -> Drop -> Breakdown -> Build-up -> Drop -> Outro. The goal is to maximize the impact of the bass and rhythms.

3. The Storyteller (AAA)

Common in Folk and Blues, this structure relies on repeating verses with small variations. It places the focus entirely on the lyrics and the performance.
