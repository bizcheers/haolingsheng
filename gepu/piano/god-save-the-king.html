📖 Historical Background

God Save the King — 'God Save the King/Queen' is the national anthem of the United Kingdom, first published in 1744. Its origins are debated — composers including Henry Carey and John Bull have been suggested. It is one of the oldest and most widely recognised national anthems in the world, and its stately melody has influenced national anthems across the globe.

Composer: Traditional British (published 1744)

🔬 Musical Structure

In 3/4 time with a stately, anthem-like character. The arrangement features the melody in the right hand above a steady harmonic accompaniment in the left. The dotted rhythms give it its characteristic royal dignity.

🎵 Piano Performance Tips

  • The dotted rhythms are crucial — they give the anthem its characteristic stately feel. Practice them precisely.
  • Keep a noble, unhurried tempo. This anthem should feel dignified and confident, never rushed.
  • The left-hand chords should be firm and steady — they underpin the processional character of the whole piece.
