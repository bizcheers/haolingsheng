The Rosetta Stone of Music

Whether you are a songwriter, a producer, or a pianist, the **Circle of Fifths** is the single most powerful tool you can have in your studio. It maps out the relationships between different keys and chords in a way that makes complex theory feel like a simple puzzle.

How to Use the Circle

Moving clockwise on the circle adds a sharp to the key signature (a jump of a perfect fifth). Moving counter-clockwise adds a flat (a jump of a perfect fourth). This is why it is often called the "Circle of Fourths and Fifths."

  • Find Relative Minors: The inner circle shows the minor key that shares the same signature as its major counterpart.
  • Chord Progressions: Chords next to each other on the circle are harmonically related and sound great together (like C, F, and G).
  • Key Changes: Use the circle to find "pivot chords" for smooth modulations.
