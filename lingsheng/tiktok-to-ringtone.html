Turning TikTok Videos into Ringtones

Discovered a viral sound on TikTok? Here is how to extract that audio and set it as your personalized alert or ringtone.

TikTok is a goldmine for audio, but downloading sounds directly can sometimes result in low-fidelity or compressed audio. For the best result, use a high-quality downloader that extracts the Original Video. If the creator has disabled downloads, use a screen recording.

The Conversion Process

  • Extract Audio: Use an "Audio Extractor" app or website. On iPhone, you can use the built-in Files app to rename the extension from `.mp4` to `.m4a` in some cases, or use a converter.
  • Trim to 30s: Ringtones must be under 30 seconds (especially for iOS). Most video converters allow you to set start/end times.
  • Save as Ringtone: For iPhone, the final file must be `.m4r`. For Android, `.mp3` is perfect.

iOS Special Method: GarageBand

If you're on an iPhone, you don't need a computer at all:

  1. 1 Download the TikTok video or record your screen.
  2. 2 Use a conversion app (like "Video to MP3") or the built-in Files app to rename the extension from .mp4 to .m4a in some cases, or use a dedicated converter tool.
  3. 3 Open GarageBand and create a "Recorder" project.
  4. 4 Tap the "Track View" icon and use the Loop Browser to import your extracted audio file.
  5. 5 Share the project as a Ringtone. GarageBand handles all the conversion for you!

Why do some TikTok sounds sound "bad"?

TikTok compresses audio heavily. If you're extracting a sound that has already been re-uploaded several times, it might sound "crushed." Always try to find the "Original Sound" source for the best quality.
