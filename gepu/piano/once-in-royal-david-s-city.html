📖 Historical Background

Once in Royal David's City — 'Once in Royal David's City' was written by Cecil Frances Alexander (words, 1848) and Henry John Gauntlett (music, 1849). It is traditionally the opening carol at the Festival of Nine Lessons and Carols from King's College, Cambridge, where a single chorister sings the first verse unaccompanied.

Composer: Henry John Gauntlett (1849).

🔬 Musical Structure

In 4/4 time with a gentle, hymn-like character. The melody flows smoothly through clear four-bar phrases. The two-voice piano arrangement adds harmonic depth while maintaining the simplicity of the original hymn.

🎵 Piano Performance Tips

  • Keep a gentle, flowing touch throughout.
  • The character should be tender and reflective.
  • The left hand provides steady harmonic support—keep it soft. Practice the longer phrases as a single musical breath.
