The Science of Social Frequency

Gospel music is deeply social—it's meant to be heard in a room full of people. This means the arrangements often have a lot of "clutter" in the 500Hz to 1kHz range. When we remaster a gospel song ringtone, we apply a subtle Bell Curve cut in this area. This allows the high-frequency "sheen" of the vocals and the low-end punch of the kick drum to stand out, making the ringtone sound "larger than life" on your phone.

The Power of the Vocal Loop

When selecting a Black Gospel ringtone, the most effective clips are usually 4-8 bar vocal runs. Because the human brain is evolutionarily hard-wired to pay attention to human voices, a vocal-centric Gospel ringtone is scientifically one of the easiest alerts to distinguish from background environmental noise.
