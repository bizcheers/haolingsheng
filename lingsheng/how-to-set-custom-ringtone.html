How to Make Your Own Ringtone: Any Song on iPhone & Android

Stop settling for default sounds. Whether it's a favorite track, a voice memo, or a Custom AI creation, here is how to get custom ringtones on your device in 2026.

Downloading a ringtone is a thing of the past. Today, the most popular way to personalize your phone is to make any song your ringtone directly on your device. In this guide, we'll cover the professional methods for both iOS and Android, ensuring your "Good Sound" (Hao Ling Sheng) is truly unique.

How to Get Custom Ringtones on iPhone Using GarageBand

Apple makes it tricky to add files from a computer, but you can do it entirely on your iPhone using the free GarageBand app.

  • Download your audio: Save your MP3 file (from our Free Samples page) to the "Files" app on your iPhone.
  • Open GarageBand: Create a new "Audio Recorder" project.
  • Import the Loop: Tap the "Tracks" icon, then the "Loop Browser" (omega symbol). Select "Files" and drag your song into the timeline.
  • Trim & Export: Ensure it's under 30 seconds. Tap the downward arrow -> "My Songs". Long-press your project and select Share -> Ringtone.
  • Set it: Go to Settings -> Sounds & Haptics -> Ringtone to select your new creation.

How to Set a Song as a Ringtone on Android

Android is much more straightforward. You don't need additional apps to set a song as ringtone on Android.

  • Download the song: Download any MP3 file directly to your phone.
  • Move to Ringtones: Use your "Files" or "My Files" app to move the MP3 into the internal folder named Ringtones.
  • Adjust Settings: Open Settings -> Sounds and vibration -> Ringtone. Your song should now appear in the list. Tap the "+" icon if it doesn't show up automatically.

Pro Production Tips for Ringtones

As a music production site, we don't just want you to have a ringtone‚Äîwe want it to sound professional. Follow these tips:

  • Fade In (3s): Phones often have a slight delay when playback starts. A short fade-in prevents the audio from "popping" in aggressively.
  • The EQ Secret: Phone speakers are small and struggle with sub-bass. Boost the 2kHz - 5kHz range slightly to make the ringtone pierce through background noise.
  • Peak Normalization: Normalize your audio to -1.0dB. This ensures maximum volume without digital clipping.
