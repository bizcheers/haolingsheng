What is Spotify Verification?

That small blue checkmark next to an artist's name on Spotify isn't just for stars like Taylor Swift or Drake. Any independent artist can get verified. Verification proves to fans, labels, and playlist curators that you are the official owner of your profile. More importantly, it unlocks Spotify for Artists, a suite of tools essential for managing your career.

The Main Requirement

Unlike Instagram or Twitter, which have complex follow-count requirements, Spotify verification is simple: You must have music live on Spotify.

As soon as your first single is delivered to Spotify by your distributor (DistroKid, TuneCore, etc.), you are eligible to claim your profile and get the blue checkmark.

Step-by-Step Guide to Verification

Step 1: Claim Your Profile

Wait until your first song is live. Then, go to artists.spotify.com and click "Get Access."

Alternatively, most distributors have a "Special Access" section that allows you to claim your profile with a single click. This is the fastest method.

Step 2: Sign In with Your Personal Account

You will need a standard personal Spotify account to "manage" your artist profile. Log in to your personal account when prompted.

Step 3: Provide Verification Proof

If you don't use a distributor's "instant access" link, you may be asked to link your social media accounts (like Instagram or Twitter) to prove you are who you say you are. This helps Spotify prevent profile hijacking.

Step 4: Wait for the Badge

Verification is usually automatic once your claim is approved. The blue checkmark should appear on your public profile within 24-48 hours.

Benefits of Being Verified

  • Edit Your Profile: You can change your profile picture, bio, and add "Artist Pick" images and links.
  • Pitch to Playlists: Verification is a prerequisite for using the official Editorial Playlist pitching tool.
  • Deep Analytics: See exactly who is listening, where they live, and which playlists are driving your growth.
  • Promotional Tools: Access features like "Marquee" or "Discovery Mode" to push your music to more listeners.
  • Concert Listings: Link your Ticketmaster or Songkick account to show your tour dates directly on your profile.

Conclusion

Verification is the first step toward becoming a professional artist on Spotify. It's free, relatively easy, and provides the foundation for all your future marketing efforts. If you have music live and you haven't claimed your profile yet, do it today!
