Creating a 3D Mix

Stereo imaging is the art of placing sounds within the horizontal (left-to-right) and depth (front-to-back) planes of a mix. A mix with poor imaging sounds "cluttered" and "small," while a great image feels wide, immersive, and detailed.

The Foundation: L-C-R Panning

One of the most powerful imaging techniques is the "L-C-R" method. This involves placing elements only and exclusively in the **Left (100%)**, **Center (0%)**, or **Right (100%)** positions. This creates a massive sense of width by leaving the "in-between" spaces completely clear for effects and detail.

Advanced Imaging Tools

  • Mid-Side EQ: Boost the high frequencies on the "Sides" (the signal that is different between L and R) to make a sound feel wider without affecting its center punch.
  • The Haas Effect: Delay one side of a signal by 15-30 milliseconds. The brain perceives this as a single, ultra-wide sound source.
  • Stereo Widening Plugins: Tools like the Ozone Imager or Polyverse Comet can artificially create width by manipulating the phase of the signal.

Always check your mix in **Mono**. If your stereo-widened tracks disappear or sound "hollow" when summed to mono, you have phase cancellation issues. Use a correlation meter to ensure your width isn't breaking your mix's integrity.
