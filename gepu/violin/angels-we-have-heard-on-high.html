📖 Historical Background

Angels We Have Heard on High — This French carol dates to the 18th century, originating in the French Alps. The famous 'Gloria in excelsis Deo' refrain with its long, flowing melisma is one of the most recognisable passages in Christmas music. The violin's singing tone in the upper register is ideally suited to the soaring 'Gloria' phrase.

Composer: Traditional French Carol (18th century)

🔬 Musical Structure

In 4/4 time, alternating between a stately verse and the cascading 'Gloria' chorus. The violin can showcase its lyrical upper register in the long descending scale of the refrain — one of the most beloved phrases in Christmas music.

🎵 Violin Performance Tips

  • The 'Gloria' melisma is the highlight — practice it slowly with smooth bow strokes before adding tempo.
  • Use a connected, singing bow stroke (détaché) for the verse and a lighter, flowing stroke for the 'Gloria' runs.
  • Keep the left hand relaxed for the scale passages — tension will cause the notes to sound scratchy.
