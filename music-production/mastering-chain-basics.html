The Final 10 Percent

Mastering is the bridge between a finished mix and a commercial release. Its goal is to ensure the song translates well to all playback systems (from iPhones to club PA systems) and reaches the standard loudness levels of the industry.

A Classic Mastering Signal Chain

While every song is unique, most professional mastering chains follow this logical order:

  1. Corrective EQ: Removing any final "mud" or problematic resonances using a high-precision digital EQ.
  2. Glue Compression: Using a slow-attack, low-ratio compressor (like an SSL Bus Comp) to "tie" the instruments together.
  3. Saturation & Excitement: Adding subtle harmonic richness and high-end "air."
  4. Stereo Enhancement: Ensuring the mix has appropriate width and mono compatibility.
  5. Limiting & Metering: Bringing the track up to commercial volume (LUFS) while ensuring it doesn't "true-peak" clip.

The Importance of Monitoring

Mastering is only as good as the room you hear it in. Always use high-quality, flat-response monitors or headphones, and frequently compare your master to a "reference track" from a professional artist in the same genre.
