📖 Historical Background

Home on the Range — 'Home on the Range' has origins in a poem written by Dr. Brewster Higley in 1872, titled 'My Western Home'. Daniel E. Kelley set it to music around the same time. It became one of the most famous American folk songs and was the favourite song of President Franklin D. Roosevelt. It is the state song of Kansas.

Composer: Daniel E. Kelley / Dr. Brewster Higley (c.1872)

🔬 Musical Structure

In 3/4 waltz time with a gentle, nostalgic character. The melody unfolds in long, lyrical phrases that suit the guitar's warm tone. The arrangement sits in first position for easy accessibility.

🎵 Guitar Performance Tips

  • Capture the nostalgic, wide-open character with a warm, sustained fingerpicking tone.
  • The 3/4 feel should flow gently — think of wide open prairies, not a strict dance.
  • The long phrases need a consistent, unhurried tempo. Rushing breaks the meditative quality of the piece.
