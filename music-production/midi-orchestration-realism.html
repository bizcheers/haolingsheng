Beyond the Keyboard

Writing for a virtual orchestra requires a different mindset than playing a piano. To achieve realism, you must treat every MIDI track as a living, breathing performer. If a violin note is static, it sounds like a machine; if it "swells," it sounds like music.

The Secret Sauce: CC Automation

The key to realistic strings, brass, and woodwinds is "Continuous Controller" (CC) automation. The most important MIDI CC channels are:

  • CC1 (Modulation): Usually controls dynamic crossfading (switching between soft and loud samples).
  • CC7 (Volume): Controls the overall level.
  • CC11 (Expression): Used to add "swells" and dynamic movement *within* a note.

Layering for Cinematic Impact

Professional film scores rarely use just one sample library. To get a "Hollywood" sound, try layering three different string libraries: one for the "attack" (shorts), one for the "lushness" (longs), and one solo violin for the "detail."

Avoid "quantizing" everything to 100%. Orchestras don't play perfectly on the beat. Shift your MIDI notes slightly off the grid (humanize) to create that natural, slightly imperfect ensemble feel.
