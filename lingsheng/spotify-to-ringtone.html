How to Make a Spotify Song Your Ringtone

Love a track on Spotify? While you can't download it directly as an MP3, there are legal ways to turn that hook into your next ringtone.

Important: Spotify encrypts its cache files. You cannot simply copy a file from the app. This guide focuses on recording and local file conversion for personal use.

Method 1: Using Screen Recording (iPhone & Android)

This is the fastest "universal" method that doesn't require a computer.

  1. 1 Record the Clip: Open Spotify, find your favorite 30-second section, and use your phone's built-in Screen Recorder.
  2. 2 Extract Audio: Use a free app like "Video to MP3" (Android) or the "Shortcuts" app (iOS) to strip the video and save just the audio.
  3. 3 Follow Setup: Use our Standard Setup Guide to apply it.

Method 2: The Best Quality (Desktop)

If you want high-fidelity sound, recording through a computer is recommended.

  1. Audio Hijack or Audacity: Use these tools (Audio Hijack for Mac, Audacity for Windows) to record the system audio while playing Spotify.
  2. Trim: Highlight the best 30 seconds (usually the chorus) and export as **WAV** or **MP3**.
  3. Transfer: Send the file to your phone via AirDrop, iCloud, or Google Drive.

Why is it difficult?

Spotify uses DRM (Digital Rights Management) to prevent piracy. This means even "Premium" downloads are protected files. To use a song as a ringtone, the audio must be in a non-protected format like MP3, M4A, or M4R (iPhone).

Vocal Removal Tip

Want just the beat? Use an AI stem splitter like Lalal.ai or Moises on your recording to remove the vocals and create a professional-sounding instrumental ringtone.
