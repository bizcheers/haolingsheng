📖 Historical Background

We Wish You a Merry Christmas — 'We Wish You a Merry Christmas' is a 16th-century English carol from the West Country, where carollers would demand figgy pudding as payment for their singing. Its bright, dance-like melody and the insistent 'good tidings' refrain make it an ideal piece for the flute, whose cheerful timbre perfectly captures the carolling spirit.

Composer: Traditional English Carol (16th century)

🔬 Musical Structure

In 3/4 time with a lively, dance-like character. The verse and 'good tidings' refrain contrast well. The arrangement sits in G major, placing the melody in the flute's bright middle register.

🎵 Flute Performance Tips

  • Keep the 3/4 feel light and dance-like — think of a cheerful jig, not a solemn waltz.
  • Use a light, crisp articulation (single tonguing) to give the carol its festive energy.
  • The 'good tidings' refrain moves more quickly — practice it separately until the rhythm feels natural before connecting with the verse.
