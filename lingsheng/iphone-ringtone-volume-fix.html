iPhone Ringtone Volume Decreases Itself?

Is your iPhone ringtone starting loud and then suddenly becoming quiet? It's likely not a bug, but a "feature" called Attention Aware.

The Culprit: Attention Aware Features

Since the introduction of FaceID, iPhones can detect when you are looking at your screen. If your phone rings and you pick it up to look at it, the iPhone assumes you are now "aware" of the call and automatically lowers the volume to save your ears (and the battery).

The Instant Fix:

  1. Open Settings
  2. Tap Face ID & Passcode
  3. Find Attention Aware Features
  4. Toggle the switch to OFF
Path: Settings > Face ID & Passcode > Attention Aware Features

Other Reasons for Quiet Ringtones

If disabling Attention Aware didn't work, check these common causes:

  • Change with Buttons: Ensure your volume buttons are actually controlling the ringtone volume, not just media volume.
    Settings > Sounds & Haptics > Change with Buttons
  • Reduce Loud Sounds: Check if Headphone Safety settings are accidentally limiting your external speakers.
    Settings > Sounds & Haptics > Headphone Safety
  • Speaker Obstruction: Over time, dust and lint can clog the bottom speaker grilles, significantly muffling ringtones.

Pro Tip: Frequency Balancing

Sometimes the "quiet" feeling is due to the ringtone file itself. If your ringtone is mostly bass, it won't project well from small iPhone speakers. Try using ringtones with strong **Mid-Range** and **High-End** presence (like our Loudest Ringtones Collection).
