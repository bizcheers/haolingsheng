A Sonic Phenomenon

If you've listened to charts in the last two years, you've heard it: that deep, percussive, woody bass that seems to "thump" rather than just hum. That is the **Log Drum**, the defining element of South African **Amapiano**.

What is it, really?

In Amapiano, the "Log Drum" isn't an acoustic drum. It's a specific synthesis technique, often created using Frequency Modulation (FM). Producers famously used the "Log Drum" preset in FL Studio's **Fruity DX10** to create this iconic sound.

How to Make It

To recreate it in any synth, you need a quick pitch envelope on your oscillator. The pitch should drop rapidly from a high frequency to a low one, creating that "knock" or "donk" sound. Pair this with a sine-based FM operator for the weight, and you have the Amapiano soul.
