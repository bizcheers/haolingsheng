What Does EPK Mean?

EPK stands for Electronic Press Kit. Think of it as your professional resume or portfolio as an artist. It‚Äôs a digital package that provides promoters, venue owners, journalists, and labels with everything they need to know about you in one concise place.

Why You Need an EPK in 2026

In a world of short attention spans, no one has time to hunt through your social media profiles to find your bio or high-res photos. An EPK makes it easy for industry professionals to "say yes" to your project. It shows that you are professional, organized, and ready for serious business.

6 Essential Elements of a Great EPK

  1. Short & Long Bio: A catchy 1-paragraph summary and a more detailed narrative about your journey.
  2. High-Resolution Photos: Both portrait (for press) and landscape (for headers).
  3. Music Samples: Embed your top tracks from SoundCloud or Spotify.
  4. Live Video: Nothing sells a booker like seeing you actually perform.
  5. Press & Reviews: Screenshots of blogs or features you've received.
  6. Contact Information: How to reach your management or booking agent.

Where to Host Your EPK

Do not send a 50MB PDF attachment. Instead, use a dedicated landing page. You can use services like Bandzoogle, Sonicbids, or simply create a hidden page on your own website. Check our Platform Performance Guide to see how to optimize your artist landing pages.

Final Pro Tips

  • Keep it Current: Update your EPK every time you release a new single or hit a milestone.
  • Mobile Friendly: Many promoters will be looking at your EPK on their phones at a venue.
  • Social Proof: Include links to your social media, but don't make them the focus. The EPK is for the industry, not the fans.
