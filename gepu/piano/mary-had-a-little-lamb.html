📖 Historical Background

Mary Had a Little Lamb — 'Mary Had a Little Lamb' was written by Sarah Josepha Hale and first published in 1830, inspired by a real incident involving a girl named Mary Sawyer. It was the first song ever recorded by Thomas Edison's phonograph in 1877.

Composer: Sarah Josepha Hale (1830).

🔬 Musical Structure

In 4/4 time with a simple, cheerful character. Built on a repeating three-note descending motif (E-D-C), the melody is one of the simplest in classical music education. The two-voice arrangement adds a harmonic dimension.

🎵 Piano Performance Tips

  • Keep the touch light and even.
  • The melody is simple but should be played musically—phrase each group of notes as a single gesture.
  • The left hand provides clear harmonic signposts. Do not rush; clarity is more important than speed.
