Rick Rubin

The Zen Master of Reduction

The Legend Behind the Hits

Frederick Jay 'Rick' Rubin is an American record executive and record producer. He is the co-founder of Def Jam Recordings and also established American Recordings. Rubin helped popularize hip hop music.

Rubin is known for a stripped-down sound, often referred to as 'dry'. He avoids heavy reverb and unnecessary instrumentation, preferring to capture the authentic performance of the artist in a room.

Notable Productions

Some of the most iconic tracks produced by Rick Rubin include:

  • Walk This Way - Run-D.M.C. ft. Aerosmith
  • Hurt - Johnny Cash
  • 99 Problems - Jay-Z
  • Under the Bridge - Red Hot Chili Peppers
  • Chop Suey! - System of a Down

Impact on Music Industry

Rubin has successfully produced for artists across metal, hip hop, rock, and country. His work with Johnny Cash on the 'American Recordings' series is legendary for revitalizing Cash's career.
